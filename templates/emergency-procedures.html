Emergency Procedures

STEP 1: Stay calm

After drowning, your child may lose consciousness or breath; you must remain calm and act quickly to ensure your own and your child's safety.

STEP 2: Call for help immediately

Call the person next to you for help and dial 000 or 112 as soon as possible, so that you can give initial first aid at the same time while help is on the way.

STEP 3: Remove the child from the water

If it is safe to do so, remove the child from the water quickly. If possible, use floats or ropes to help to avoid getting into danger again yourself.

STEP 4: Check for breathing and heartbeat

As soon as your child is rescued to shore, check their breathing and pulse. If the child is not breathing or has a heartbeat, prepare to perform cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR).

STEP 5: Perform cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR)

1.Chest compressions: Lay the child flat on a hard surface with hands folded over the center of the chest and apply 100-120 compressions per minute to a depth of about 4-5 cm.
2.Artificial respiration: After every 30 compressions, perform 2 artificial respirations, pinch the child's nose and blow mouth-to-mouth to see if the chest rises and falls.
3.Continued First Aid: Continue chest compressions and artificial respiration until first aiders arrive.

STEP 6: Keep warm and wait for help

While waiting for help, make sure your child stays warm to avoid further heat loss. Wrap the child in a blanket or dry clothing, but avoid moving them excessively
